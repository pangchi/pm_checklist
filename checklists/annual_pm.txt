# Annual Preventive Maintenance Checklist
# Frequency: Yearly | Est. Duration: Full day (8–12 hours)

SECTION: Pre-Shutdown Planning

NOTE: Annual PM requires full equipment shutdown. Coordinate with production planning at least 1 week in advance.
STEP: Shutdown plan reviewed and approved by engineering
STEP: All required spare parts and materials confirmed on-hand
STEP_VALUE: Record planned shutdown date and time
STEP: Permit-to-work issued and signed by safety officer
STEP: Lock Out / Tag Out (LOTO) applied — all energy sources isolated
PHOTO: Photograph all LOTO tags and isolation points

SECTION: Full Disassembly Inspection

STEP: Remove all access panels and covers
STEP: Inspect all internal structural components for cracks, corrosion, or fatigue
STEP_VALUE: Record any structural defects found (None / Minor / Major)
STEP: Inspect all internal wiring harnesses — replace if chafed or brittle
STEP: Photograph and document all wear-critical components before replacement
PHOTO: Photograph internal components before cleaning

SECTION: Complete Mechanical Overhaul

STEP: Replace all scheduled-replacement wear parts per OEM annual list
STEP_VALUE: Record all parts replaced (attach parts list if needed)
STEP: Replace all bearings on annual replacement schedule
STEP_VALUE: Record bearing part numbers replaced
STEP: Replace all drive belts and couplings
STEP: Replace all seals, O-rings, and gaskets throughout
STEP: Replace all filters (air, oil, hydraulic, process)
STEP_VALUE: Record total number of filters replaced
PHOTO: Photograph replaced components

SECTION: Full Electrical Service

STEP: Inspect and clean all control panels — vacuum dust from enclosures
STEP: Inspect and re-torque all terminal block connections
STEP_VALUE: Measure insulation resistance on all motor windings (MΩ)
STEP: Test and verify all safety relays and interlocks
STEP: Replace any UPS or backup batteries as per schedule
STEP: Perform thermographic scan of all electrical panels if available
PHOTO: Photograph electrical panels after service

SECTION: Fluid System Full Drain & Refill

STEP: Drain all hydraulic, lubrication, and cooling fluids completely
STEP: Flush all fluid circuits before refilling
STEP_VALUE: Record flush fluid type and volume (L)
STEP: Refill with fresh fluids per OEM specification
STEP_VALUE: Record new fluid type, grade, and volume (L)
STEP: Bleed and purge all air from fluid systems
STEP_VALUE: Record system pressure after refill (bar / PSI)

SECTION: Calibration & Instrumentation

NOTE: All instruments must be calibrated by a certified technician or external lab.
STEP: Submit all process instruments for annual calibration
STEP_VALUE: Record calibration certificate numbers (attach copies)
STEP: Update calibration stickers on all instruments
STEP: Test and calibrate all safety pressure relief and overpressure devices
STEP_VALUE: Record relief valve set pressure verified (bar / PSI)
PHOTO: Photograph calibration stickers on instruments

SECTION: Reassembly & Torque Verification

STEP: Reassemble all components per OEM torque specifications
STEP_VALUE: Record torque wrench ID / calibration date used
STEP: Verify all fastener torques with secondary check
STEP: Reinstall all guards, covers, and access panels
STEP: Verify all warning labels and safety signs are in place

SECTION: Commissioning & Performance Verification

STEP: Remove LOTO — follow lockout removal procedure
STEP: Power on and verify startup sequence — no abnormal alarms
STEP_VALUE: Record startup time (seconds)
STEP: Run equipment at low speed / partial load — check for leaks, noise, vibration
STEP_VALUE: Record vibration level at key measurement points (mm/s RMS)
STEP: Ramp to full operating conditions
STEP_VALUE: Record peak operating temperature (°C)
STEP_VALUE: Record operating current at full load (A)
STEP_VALUE: Record cycle time at full load (seconds)
STEP: Run for minimum 2-hour soak test — monitor all parameters
STEP_VALUE: Record soak test duration (hours)
STEP: Verify all alarms, interlocks, and safety devices are functional
STEP: Confirm handshake with control system / MES
PHOTO: Photograph equipment operating at full load

SECTION: Documentation & Sign-Off

STEP: All replaced parts, fluids, and calibrations logged in maintenance system
STEP: Maintenance report completed and attached to work order
STEP: All tools, equipment, and temporary fixtures removed from work area
STEP: Area cleaned and restored to normal operating condition
STEP_VALUE: Record total maintenance duration (hours)
PHOTO: Photograph final equipment condition — all panels closed
STEP: Report reviewed and signed off by maintenance supervisor
STEP: Equipment formally handed back to production
